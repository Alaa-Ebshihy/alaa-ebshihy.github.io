Alaa El-Ebshihy

My name is Alaa El-Ebshihy, and I am currently a researcher at Research Studios Austria (Data Science Studio) and a PhD candidate in Computer Science at TU Wien. With a research background in Information Retrieval (IR) and Natural Language Processing (NLP), focusing mainly on analyzing textual change over time, a topic that has been central to both my master’s and PhD theses.

Currently, I'm in my last stages of my PhD is under the supervision of Prof. Andreas Rauber and Dr. Florina Piroi. My research focuses on identifying and representing knowledge deltas in scientific literature, which looks at methods to extract information from literature to represent differences between research articles.

In addition to my research, I have been actively involved in the organization of the LongEval Lab. My role spans multiple stages including proposal writing, dataset preparation, collecting submissions, evaluating the participating systems, and organizing the corresponding workshop sessions during the CLEF conference.

I also contribute to the academic community through reviewing activities for several conferences such as SIGIR, ICTIR, ECIR, and CLEF

Beyond research, I have accumulated industry experience through roles in software development across various companies including Amazon Web Services, Softxpert, Webalo Inc, and Novomind Egypt LLC.
